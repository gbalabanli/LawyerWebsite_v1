Signing quickly can create long-term legal exposure. Before signing, verify who is bound by the agreement, what each side must deliver, and what happens when deadlines are missed.

## Scope and obligations

The contract should state deliverables in measurable terms. Vague language often leads to interpretation disputes.

## Payment and penalties

Confirm payment terms, tax treatment, late-fee conditions, and any unilateral right to change price.

## Exit and dispute clause

Termination and dispute resolution clauses decide how conflicts are handled. Review them before any signature.
