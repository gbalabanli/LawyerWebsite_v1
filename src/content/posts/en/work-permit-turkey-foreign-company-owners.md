Foreign entrepreneurs often open a company in Turkey and assume that ownership allows them to work in that company. This assumption can be risky. Company ownership, management authority, residence status, and work authorization are related but separate legal issues.

If a foreign founder will actively manage, represent, or work for the company in Turkey, work permit planning should be reviewed before operations begin.

## Separate Ownership from Work Authorization

Owning shares in a Turkish company does not automatically create the right to work in Turkey. A passive investor and an active manager are not the same. If the foreigner signs contracts, manages staff, performs services, handles clients, or runs daily operations in Turkey, work authorization may be required.

The risk is not only theoretical. Work without proper authorization can create administrative fines, immigration problems, future residence permit issues, and business disruption. It can also affect employees, contracts, and bank or compliance relationships.

Foreign founders should define their role clearly before incorporation or immediately after setup.

## Review Company Eligibility and Role

Work permit strategy depends on the company and the person's role. A newly established company, a company with employees, a founder-manager, a technical specialist, and a foreign shareholder living abroad may all require different analysis.

The company should review capital, activity field, tax records, financial status, workplace address, employment structure, and whether the foreigner's role is commercially justified. If the company was formed only recently, the timing of the application may need careful planning.

The role description should match reality. A vague job title or inconsistent contract can weaken the file.

## Prepare Employer and Foreigner Documents

Work permit applications require both employer-side and foreigner-side documents. These may include company registry records, tax documents, financial statements, employment contract, passport copy, photograph, diploma or professional documents where relevant, and role information.

Foreign documents may need translation. Passport validity should be checked because short remaining validity can create timing problems.

If the foreigner is already in Turkey, residence status matters. If the application is made from abroad, consular reference and timing may be relevant. The route should be selected before documents are prepared.

## Coordinate Residence and Permit Timing

A work permit can affect residence status, but the timing must be managed. A foreigner with an expiring residence permit, pending application, overstay risk, or travel plans should coordinate these issues before filing.

Company owners should also plan renewals. A first permit and later extensions may require different evidence. If the company changes address, manager, shareholding, or activity, the work permit file may need updates.

For foreign company owners, the safest approach is to connect corporate setup with immigration planning. The company should be formed to operate, but the founder's right to work should be addressed separately and documented properly.
